The Basics

This is a Treble Clef. It is the most commonly used clef for instuments.
It has five lines and four spaces for notes to sit on.

This is a Bass Clef. It is mostly used in piano music for the left hand and
also has five lines and four spaces for notes.
Just for now, we are going to stick to the treble clef since it is used the most.

Notes For Treble Clef

This is C major scale. It is played by using the notes; C,D,E,F,G,A,B and C.

This is D major scale. It is played by using; D,E,F#,G,A,B,C# and D.

This is E major scale. It is played with; E,F#,G#,A,B,C#,D# and E.

This is F major scale. It includes; F,G,A,Bb,C,D,E and F.

This is G major scale. It has the notes; G,A,B, C, D, E, F#, and G.

This is A major scale. It is played with; A,B,C#,D,E,F#,G# and A.

This is B major scale. It is played by using; B,C#,D#,E,F#,G#,A# and B.
